## The positioning

Lou is a builder who resolves messy problems into one clear, shipped answer. The brand name says it. Entity resoLOUtion. You take scattered data, scattered tools, and scattered ideas, and you resolve them into a working product.

This is the through line for the headline, the about section, and every post. Not a job title. A promise. You take the mess and you hand back the resolution.

Proof that backs the promise.

- Truss Home. An AI coordinated home services marketplace, live on Vercel, with payments, dispatch, and four booking chats.
- ESP Development. A live client site for a North Jersey design build firm, pending permission to feature.
- Jets / PDL demo. A sales demo that enriches fan ticketing data with identity signals, all data fabricated and labeled.

## What a turnaround means here

Three problems most builder profiles have, and the fix for each.

1. The profile describes a person instead of selling an outcome. Fix. Lead every section with what the reader gets, not with a title.
2. The profile has no single next step. Fix. One call to action everywhere, book a call, pointing at the site.
3. The profile shows no proof. Fix. Put shipped work in the featured section and in the experience entries, with links.

## Content plan

The profile is the storefront. Posts are the foot traffic. Keep a simple, steady cadence rather than a big launch.

Cadence. Two posts a week to start. One build in public, one lesson or opinion.

Post types that fit the positioning.

- Build in public. A short story on something you shipped this week, with a screenshot.
- Before and after. A messy problem and the resolved version, the literal brand promise.
- Teardown. A quick read on a product or workflow, what works, what you would fix.
- Proof drop. A case study from Truss Home or a demo, ending with book a call.

Rules for every post.

- One idea per post.
- A clear takeaway in the first two lines, since that is all the feed shows.
- End with a soft call to action that points to the site when it fits, not every time.
- No hashtags, no fluff, plain language.

---

## The funnel

LinkedIn profile or post, then the site, then the showcase, then book a call, then a booked discovery call.

The profile feeds the site in three places. The headline, the featured section, and the banner. The site closes with the single call to action. Wire analytics so you can see which posts and which profile views turn into booked calls.
